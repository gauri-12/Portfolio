Introduction

Dataset Description

The FBI provides data on the number of firearm checks by month, state, and type. The NICS conducts background checks on people who want to own a firearm or explosive, as required by law.That background check verifies the buyer does not have a criminal record or isn't otherwise ineligible to purchase or own a firearm.

Relationship between the tables:

The gun_data contains the states and the background check information by Month and year. The US_Census_Data has the corresponding demographic information for these states for various years. Combining these two using the 'state' and the 'year' would help explore the data better and derive more meaningful insights.

Question(s) for Analysis

  1. What are the overall trends in the total background checks over time?
  2. Which states have the highest and the lowest total background check? (Top and bottom 10)
  3. Are there specific months or seasons that show a significant increase or decrease in totals background checks?
  4. Is there a relationship between a state's population and the number of totals background checks?
  5. Are there any observable relationships between specific demographic facts from the US_Census_Data and the types of firearms being checked in different states?

Conclusions

1. Overall Trends

Through the years, the total number of firearm background checks does not always show a steady increasing trends for all the states. There are sudden spikes in the number at random time periods, unique to each of the states.

2. North Carolina permit spike

The state of North Carolina (state with the highest number of total background checks), saw a substantial increase in permit checks in North Carolina during early 2014. However, this was not correlated with significant changes in the state's population or housing unit data. This suggests that the spike was likely influenced by other factors.

3. Seasonality check

The analysis of total background checks over time indicates a clear seasonal pattern, with consistent peaks observed during the months of November/December and January. This aligns with the US Holiday season (Thanksgiving/ Christmas and New Year).

4. Correlation analysis

A correlation analysis was performed across 6 major demographic factors and corresponding totals for the various types of firearms checked. This revealed that the 'Population estiamte' demographic information has a strong positive correaltion with 4 out of 5 types of firearm background checks, namely - Handgun checks, Long gun checks, Other checks and Total checks. This strongly indicates that the overall population size within an area is a key contributing factor to the volume of background checks conducted.

Limitations

While the analysis successfully identified the permit spike and ruled out population and housing changes as causes, it could not pinpoint the actual reason behind this anomaly.This suggests that the census dataset used lacks crucial information, such as state-specific policy changes, socio-economic factors, or significant events that might have influenced firearm purchasing and permitting trends during that period.
